Our Heritage & Mission

Mudiali Club Durga Puja, established in 1930, stands as one of South Kolkata‚Äôs most iconic celebrations. Over the years, it has grown into a landmark puja that beautifully blends cultural tradition with modern creativity. What makes it truly unique is its dedication to social and environmental responsibility, which has earned it recognition across the city and beyond.

The club is particularly admired for its eco-friendly approach. Long before sustainability became a popular concept, Mudiali Club began using natural and recycled materials to design its pandals and idols. From clay, bamboo, straw, and jute to innovative use of discarded objects, every creation reflects harmony with nature. This conscious effort has made the puja an inspiration for other organizers who aim to combine devotion with responsibility towards the environment.

Every year, the puja introduces innovative themes that are both visually striking and culturally meaningful. These concepts often highlight social issues, heritage art forms, or universal messages of harmony and peace. The artistic presentation, combined with exquisite lighting and detailed craftsmanship, turns the pandal into a space where tradition meets imagination.

Mudiali Club attracts thousands of visitors during the festive days, who come not just to offer prayers but also to experience the unique charm of its creations. Families, art lovers, and tourists admire the blend of spirituality, sustainability, and artistry that the puja offers. Today, it continues to be a shining example of how a community puja can preserve tradition while embracing modern values, making it a jewel of Kolkata‚Äôs grand Durga Puja celebrations.

Our mission extends beyond the five days of Puja - we organize cultural programs, support local artisans, and maintain the rich heritage of Bengali traditions throughout the year.
